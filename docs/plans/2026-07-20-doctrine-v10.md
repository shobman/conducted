# Doctrine v10 — the owner's attention is the scarce resource (plan of record)

**Owner ruling, 2026-07-20:** *"yes v10 — so I can ignore the principal — and start over."*

## The problem, stated precisely

v8 fixed the **CONDUCTOR→PRINCIPAL** boundary. It named two escalation classes, gave routing-class
escalations an absorption lane with conditions, and left judgment-class going to the owner. It worked.

**The PRINCIPAL→OWNER boundary has the identical disease and no cure.** Nothing in v1–v9 tells a
Principal what may NOT be queued to the owner. `HELD FOR OWNER` is defined only by what goes ON it
(judgment-class escalations, rulings, showcases) and never by what is barred from it. A Principal
facing an unruled question therefore has exactly one safe-looking move — queue it — and safety
compounds: every wake, the board grows. The dispatch board becomes a wall of governance homework and
the owner's attention, the one input that cannot be bought, is spent on items that never needed it.

## The evidence — one board, seven items, zero signal

A **queue-app** Principal wake emitted a dispatch board with **SEVEN `HELD FOR OWNER` items and ZERO ready
dispatches**. The frontier was closed and the queue was full: nothing could move without the owner,
and the owner had a wall to read. Triaged afterwards by the owner and the conducted Principal:

| # | Item as queued | What it actually was |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | "How do we land the v7+ migration when templates fall outside every lane?" | **A DOCTRINE BUG** — the migration/templates territory deadlock (diagnosed below). Blocking the queue app, the game project AND the booking app. Belongs upstream as a field note, never in the owner's queue. |
| 2 | "Do notional caps BREAK or WARN?" | **A ruling with an obvious answer the Principal could have reasoned to.** Its own money line showed `cash $0` on every row. A cap halting work that costs no money is a breaker wired to the wrong sensor. |
| 3 | "What does the $250 cover?" | **Not the queue app's number at all** — another project's leg cap, inherited by confusion. Answerable from repo state. |
| 4 | "Install a merge App that bypasses required approval?" | **A genuine security decision. Correctly held.** A trust-boundary change; only the owner may widen it. |
| 5–7 | Branch deletions · a waiver question that already carried its own recommendation · items carried forward across multiple wakes | **Chores and stale carry-forward.** Two-way doors, all of them, most already answered by the text queuing them. |
| (8) | A physical-world check unobservable from the repo | **The one truly owner-only item** — a fact only the owner could see. It resolved to a benign external cause. No defect. |

**Of seven items, one needed the owner — and that one turned out to be nothing.** The board's
signal-to-noise was effectively zero while its frontier sat closed. That is the pathology this law
exists to kill, and it is worth naming as a single sentence: **a closed frontier plus a long owner
queue is not a busy estate; it is an over-escalating Principal.**

Two items deserve a note against conducted's own record, because they cut both ways:

- **Item 2 is partly conducted's fault.** The v9 MIGRATIONS entry lists "whether notional caps BREAK
  or merely WARN" under **Conflicts** — i.e. doctrine itself labelled it an owner ruling. v10 does not
  retract that; it draws the distinction the v9 entry lacked. **Owner-TUNABLE is not owner-BLOCKING.**
  Where doctrine flags a value as the owner's to tune, the Principal adopts the reasoned default, logs
  it with its reasoning and its reversal path, and keeps working; the owner retunes at showcase. A
  tunable with no default is a stop-work order in disguise. (v10 supplies the default for this one:
  **notional caps WARN, cash caps BREAK** — the v9 plan doc itself already reasoned to it.)
- **Item 1 is entirely conducted's fault**, and it is the worked example for clause 3 below.

## The worked example — the migration/templates deadlock (a doctrine bug, mechanically confirmed)

Verified against the shipped gates in this repo, not inferred:

- `seed/.github/workflows/gates.yml:45` — a **trailerless** PR's diff must stay inside
  `docs/**`, root `CLAUDE.md`, and `work/*/brief.md`. Anything else FAILS.
- `seed/scripts/manifest-check.sh` — a **trailered** PR must stay inside its node's manifest plus
  `work/<node>/**`, `docs/findings/**`, `docs/ledger/**`, `docs/field-notes/**`.
- An adopted repo receives the seed's templates at root `templates/` and scripts at `scripts/`
  (`/principal` → Adoption step 3). **Neither path is in either set.**
- The v9 migration ritual mandates: *"ensure the ledger template carries `window_cost` / `cash_cost` /
  `models` / `fable_optin` / `window_reading` and `status.json` the `actuals` currency fields."*

So doctrine **mandates** an edit to `templates/**` that **no lane can land**: not trailerless (outside
the legal set), not as a node (templates are not node work, and `scripts/**` is behaviorally never node
territory — see the gates' own repair path). This is precisely the v6 condition — *"a gate that can
reject a mandated artifact is a seed bug by definition"* — recurring one level up, on the migration
lane rather than on a node's artifacts.

**What matters for v10 is not the fix but the routing.** Three separate projects' Principals hit this
and at least one queued it to the owner as a decision. **It is not a decision.** The owner cannot rule
his way out of a contradiction between two of conducted's own gates; asking him to is asking him to
patch a bug by opinion. The correct move — now law under clause 3 — is a field note upstream and a
local, logged, reversible workaround while it lands. v10 does not fix the deadlock (see *Deliberately
not in v10*), but it records the mechanical diagnosis above so the fix does not have to rediscover it.

## Scope

1. **The owner's queue is a scarce resource with a hard admission rule.** `HELD FOR OWNER` is
   RESERVED for five classes and closed to everything else: **irreversibles / one-way doors ·
   value and taste judgments (what a win is, does this feel right) · money increases and new caps ·
   physical-world or private-knowledge checks (facts unobservable from the repo) · genuine
   security and trust-boundary changes.** Nothing else may be queued to the owner.
2. **Everything else the Principal decides on its own judgment and LOGS** — mirroring v8's structure
   one tier up. **Reversibility is the gate, not size or importance.** The decision, its reasoning
   and its **reversal path** land in the wake notes in v8's delta-ledger shape, and the owner
   SAMPLES them at showcase through the machinery v8 already defines (risk-scaled sample, full
   reads, two verdict classes, tolerable deviation rate). No parallel system.
3. **Doctrine defects are never owner decisions.** A Principal that finds a contradiction in the
   doctrine files a field note through the existing harvest channel and applies a logged, reversible
   local workaround. It does not queue the owner to adjudicate the doctrine's own contradictions.
4. **The board has a budget: 3.** More than three held items forces a **re-triage before the board is
   emitted** — the excess is evidence of over-escalation, not of a busy estate. Explicitly:
   **the budget may only be met by absorbing NON-owner-class items.** Owner-class items are CENSUS;
   if five genuine one-way doors exist, all five ride and the board says the budget was exceeded by
   owner-class items. Demoting an owner-class item to fit the budget is the violation in clause 7.
5. **Carry-forward expires after 2 wakes.** A third wake may not simply re-print an item. It is
   **re-classed** (was it ever the owner's?), **decided** by the Principal, or **explicitly abandoned
   with a written reason**. This is a discipline on the PRINCIPAL, never a deadline on the owner —
   an item does not become urgent by ageing, and expiry never converts into a nag.
6. **The owner may ignore the board entirely, without penalty.** Parked-because-the-owner-is-living-
   his-life is a legal, non-degrading state, exactly as v9's parked-waiting-for-window is. Nothing
   nags; no work is marked failed because the owner did not look. Pull, not push — the Desk ruling,
   made law for the Principal too.
7. **Anti-abdication.** This law is not licence to decide what is merely inconvenient to ask about.
   The test is **"could ONLY the owner know or decide this?"** — never *"would asking be slower?"*
   Deciding an owner-class item is a **violation**, logged and surfaced exactly as v8 treats acting
   outside stated bounds. In doubt, it is the owner's.
8. **Under-escalation is a measured pole too.** An owner queue that is empty for several wakes on a
   moving estate is as suspicious as one that is full — paired with v8's habituation gauges, so a
   Principal cannot optimise for a score of zero.

## The two tunable numbers

**Board budget = 3 held items (owner-tunable).**
The board carried 7 items of which 1 was genuine — an empirical genuine-rate near 14%. A budget
of 3 leaves room for a genuinely eventful cycle (say a security decision, a money increase and a taste
call landing in one wake) while making a fourth item an alarm rather than a shrug. It is also below
the count at which a human stops reading a list and starts batch-approving it: the owner-habituation
gauge v8 defines fires on volume, so the budget is the upstream control for the same failure. Set at
3 rather than 1 because a hard 1 would create exactly the demotion pressure clause 7 forbids.

**Carry-forward expiry = 2 wakes (owner-tunable).**
Chosen to REUSE an existing clock rather than start a competing one. The wake ritual already treats
no-state-change as LOOPING with a two-strike response — first offense replan, second consecutive
escalates. Held items get the same cadence: two wakes of no movement is the estate's existing
definition of stuck, and applying one number to both keeps a Principal from carrying two staleness
clocks that disagree. It also matches v8's strike rule, where strike 3 is where a repeated
escalation converts class rather than repeating again.

Both live where the other owner-config values live — ruled per repo, never inferred by a session,
per v9's *"owner-set assumptions are CONFIG, never inference."* A session finding no ruled value uses
the doctrine default and says which it used; it does not invent a different one.

## How the anti-abdication clause was kept from being toothless

A prohibition with no cost is decoration. Six interlocks, five of which reuse machinery that already
exists rather than adding enforcement that does not:

1. **The test has a mechanical proxy, so it is decidable.** "Could only the owner know or decide
   this?" is operationalised as **"is the information needed to decide this OBSERVABLE FROM THE
   REPO?"** Physical world, the owner's private knowledge, his taste, his money, an irreversible
   commitment — all fall outside the repo by construction. This deliberately echoes v9's
   *observed, never queried* sensor law: the Principal reasons from what it can actually see, and
   names the boundary rather than guessing across it. **"Asking would be slower" is named in the law
   as an explicitly non-qualifying reason**, so the most likely rationalisation is pre-refuted.
2. **The budget cannot bite on owner-class items.** This is the load-bearing one. A board budget
   creates pressure to reclassify downward — the exact abdication risk — so clause 4 states that
   owner-class items are census and an over-budget board of genuine one-way doors is CORRECT and
   simply says so. The budget applies only to the non-owner-class residue. Without this, clause 4
   would weaponise clause 7's failure mode.
3. **Absorption is never silence.** Every absorbed candidate is logged with its class, the
   disqualifying test, and its reversal path — an absorbed item leaves a bigger trace than a queued
   one, which inverts the usual incentive. The log is what the owner samples.
4. **Misclassification is a violation in v8's exact sense**, not a new category: it is scored as
   *outside stated bounds*, kept separate from *inside intent but I'd have chosen differently*
   (which is coached, never punished), and it counts against the **tolerable deviation rate** —
   so a Principal whose sampled misclassifications exceed the threshold flips to 100% review next
   cycle. The existing lane re-tightens itself; nothing new had to be built.
5. **Doubt breaks toward the owner.** v8's *"when in doubt about reversibility, it is
   judgment-class"* is inherited verbatim at this boundary. A law whose ties break toward escalation
   cannot be read as licence to escalate less.
6. **Both poles are measured (clause 8).** A permanently empty owner queue on a moving estate is
   surfaced as under-escalation. A Principal cannot game the law by driving the number to zero,
   because zero is also an alarm.

The honest residue: **all six are behavioral.** No gate reads a board, a triage log, or a class
label. What these interlocks buy is that a violation is *discoverable by an owner who samples* —
not that it is *prevented*. Stated in the law itself, in v8's idiom.

## The auditability catch — the board is ephemeral

Worth stating separately because it nearly sank the whole design. **The dispatch board is terminal
output, not a committed artifact.** A law whose entire subject matter exists only in a session's
last message is unauditable by construction: the owner cannot sample what was never written down,
and a Principal that absorbed forty items would leave the same trace as one that absorbed none.

So v10 requires the triage record — absorbed items, their class, the disqualifying test, the
reversal path — to be **committed to the wake notes** (`docs/wake/<date>.md`), which already exist,
already ride the bookkeeping lane, and already land without an owner click. The showcase carries the
cycle roll-up so the sampled audit reaches it. **No new artifact, no new gate, no new lane** — and
the difference between a logged absorption and an unlogged one is now a repo fact.

it is the honest one. Claiming more would repeat exactly the overclaiming v8's audit existed to
remove.

## Wording the owner should eyeball

1. **Both numbers (3 and 2) are proposals**, argued from one board and one existing clock. They are
   the likeliest thing to be wrong and the cheapest thing to change — they are config, not law.
2. **The notional-caps default flips a v9 conflict.** v9 listed BREAK-vs-WARN as an owner ruling;
   v10 supplies **WARN for notional, BREAK for cash** as the default a Principal adopts rather than
   blocks on. If the owner prefers notional caps to keep breaking, that is one ruling and this
   default goes away — but the *pattern* (tunable ⇒ default + log, never stop-work) should survive
   either way.
3. **"Genuine security / trust-boundary change" is the least crisp of the five classes.** The
   queue app's merge-App item was correctly held under it, but a Principal could stretch it to cover routine
   permission work. The narrowing offered: it is owner-class when it WIDENS who or what can act
   without review. Worth confirming.
4. **Clause 6 has a cost, and it should be said out loud.** If the owner may ignore the board
   indefinitely and nothing nags, then genuinely owner-class items can sit forever. v10 accepts this
   deliberately — the alternative is a nagging system, which the Desk ruling rejected. The mitigation
   is that there should now be so few held items that reading them is cheap when the owner chooses to.
5. **v10 governs the Principal only.** The owner's own attention outside the board — Slack, the
   Desk, a conductor asking directly — is untouched. If over-escalation shows up on those surfaces,
   that is separate evidence for a separate law.

## Deliberately NOT in v10

- **The migration/templates deadlock fix — NOT in v10, and it no longer needs to be.** When this
  branch was cut, `law/v9-1-migration-lane` was empty. It is not now: **PR #11 (v9.1, the migration
  landing path) landed on the remote at 11:34, eight minutes before this PR** and makes
  `templates/**` trailerless-legal. Its evidence is stronger than the diagnosis above and reached it
  independently — the queue app's migration PR (a v6→v9 migration) RED and unfixable from inside itself, beside its
  docs-only twin GREEN on the same lane, isolating `templates/**` as the sole cause. It also
  draws the line v10 did not: **templates are inert scaffolding; `.github/workflows/**` and
  `scripts/**` are the measuring apparatus**, so gate-touching migrations still need the owner's
  admin lane. v10 keeps the deadlock as its worked example for clause 3 — and v9.1 is now the
  *outcome* of that clause, not merely an illustration of it: the defect was routed upstream and
  fixed on evidence, which is precisely what could never have happened in the owner's queue.
  **Merge order:** v9.1 (#11) should land first; this branch is cut from `main` and will conflict
  with it in two trivial places — the `Doctrine version:` line (v9.1 vs v10 — v10 wins) and the
  end of `docs/MIGRATIONS.md` (both append; order them v9.1 then v10).
- **A mechanical triage checker.** Nothing parses a board or a class label, and v10 does not pretend
  otherwise. A future runner could lint the wake-note triage table for unclassified items — until
  one exists, this is discipline.
- **Extending the law to conductors.** v8 governs conductor→Principal, v10 governs Principal→owner.
  Whether a conductor may absorb owner-class-looking questions is not touched; its escalations still
  reach the owner through the Principal, who now triages them.
- **A standing "owner's inbox" artifact.** Tempting, and rejected: it is a second board, and a second
  board is a second place for items to accumulate silently. The wake notes plus the showcase
  roll-up are enough.
- **Multi-repo extension** — still frozen. Noted because attention is estate-wide in the same way
  v9's window is: three repos each honouring a budget of 3 present the owner with nine. The
  per-repo budget is correct locally and wrong globally, and v10 says so rather than half-solving it.
- **Silent-completion law** — still queued; no v10 evidence forces it.
