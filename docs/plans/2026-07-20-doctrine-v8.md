## The strike rule (owner amendment on PR #8, 2026-07-20)

**Why:** the proposal's §5 escalation-storm findings name the *over-escalation pole* — "the documented
'polite, expensive, infinite ping-pong' of ownerless handoffs" (Cognition), repeat-escalation on the
same node, and the storm counter — but the counter only SENSED. The owner ruled it must ACT. So a
routing-class escalation now carries a **per-node, per-edge strike count** (edge = the specific brief
boundary being escalated about), recorded in the handoff contract and the delta ledger: **strike 1**
absorbs normally; **strike 2** (same edge) absorbs only if the Principal CHANGES THE APPROACH
(re-scoped territory, different decomposition, or different-tier model — never a same-brief re-issue)
and names what changed; **strike 3** (same edge, no state change) AUTO-CONVERTS to judgment-class →
HELD FOR OWNER regardless of reversibility or budget. The ruling's reasoning is mission-command: three
routing escalations on one edge is evidence the INTENT was miscalibrated — a judgment matter by
definition, not another absorption. Strikes are BEHAVIORAL (handoff contracts + the delta ledger,
auditable at showcase); the narrow mechanical backstop is the delta ledger's new **strike column**
(added to the showcase template), which makes an unescalated loop visible to the sampled audit. Files:
`seed/CLAUDE.md` (Scope deltas section), `seed/templates/showcase.md` (strike column),
`skills/principal/SKILL.md` (the absorb step checks the strike count first).

## Mechanical vs behavioral, stated for v8's own law

Almost the entire scope-delta lane is BEHAVIORAL — law agents follow, not something a gate reads. No
gate parses an INTENT block, scores an absorption condition, reads a delta ledger, or fires a
sensor. The mechanical backstops are narrow and named in the law: the brief/showcase templates carry
the skeletons and the placeholder gate fails unfilled SCREAMING-CASE tokens inside them; the
territory gate enforces the self-widening floor from the base ref; and in bot mode the classifier +
branch protection hold the owner-required merge tier. Everything else is conductor discipline and the
owner's sample audit — said plainly so no one mistakes this law for a machine (the audit discipline,
applied to itself).
